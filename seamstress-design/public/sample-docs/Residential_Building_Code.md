## Chapter 1: Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
These regulations establish minimum standards for residential construction within the City of Springfield to ensure public safety, structural integrity, and community standards.

### 1.2 Scope
This code applies to all residential construction including:
- New construction
- Additions and alterations
- Accessory structures (decks, sheds, carports)
- Renovations requiring structural modifications

### 1.3 Authority
Authority for these regulations is granted under Springfield Municipal Code Chapter 15, Buildings and Construction.

## Chapter 2: General Requirements

### 2.1 Permits Required
A building permit is required for any construction work that:
- Creates new living space
- Modifies structural elements
- Changes the building footprint
- Exceeds thresholds specified in this code

### 2.2 Setback Requirements - Primary Structures
All primary residential structures must maintain minimum setbacks:
- **Front yard**: 25 feet from property line
- **Rear yard**: 20 feet from property line
- **Side yards**: 10 feet from property line (each side)

### 2.3 Height Restrictions
- **Maximum building height**: 35 feet from average grade
- **Stories**: Maximum 2.5 stories for residential dwellings
- **Exceptions**: Chimneys, antennas, and architectural features may exceed by up to 10 feet

## Chapter 3: Permit Application Process

### 3.1 Application Requirements
All permit applications must include:
1. Completed application form
2. Site plan showing:
   - Property boundaries
   - Existing structures
   - Proposed construction location
   - Setback measurements
3. Construction drawings with:
   - Foundation details
   - Framing plans
   - Electrical layout
   - Plumbing layout (if applicable)
4. Material specifications
5. Estimated cost of construction
6. Contractor information (if not owner-builder)

### 3.2 Review Process
- **Initial Review**: 5-10 business days
- **Revisions**: Additional 3-5 business days per revision
- **Approval**: Permit valid for 12 months from issue date

### 3.3 Fees
Permit fees are calculated based on estimated construction value:
- Base fee: $150
- Additional cost: $15 per $1,000 of construction value
- Inspection fees: Included in permit fee

## Chapter 4: Accessory Structures

### 4.1 Definitions
**Accessory Structure**: A structure subordinate to and located on the same lot as a primary dwelling, including but not limited to:
- Detached garages
- Storage sheds
- Decks and patios
- Gazebos and pergolas
- Carports

### 4.2 Decks and Patios

#### 4.2.1 Setback Requirements
All residential decks must maintain the following minimum setbacks from property lines:
- **Rear yard**: 10 feet from property line
- **Side yard**: 5 feet from property line
- **Front yard**: 20 feet from property line

**Exception**: Decks under 30 inches in height above finished grade may extend to within 5 feet of the rear property line.

#### 4.2.2 Permit Requirements
A building permit is **required** for:
- All decks over 200 square feet in area
- All decks attached to the primary structure (regardless of size)
- All decks more than 30 inches above finished grade at any point

**No permit required** for:
- Ground-level decks under 200 square feet
- Freestanding decks under 30 inches in height and under 200 square feet

#### 4.2.3 Approved Materials
Decking materials must meet one of the following standards:

**Wood Decking**:
- Pressure-treated lumber rated for ground contact (minimum .40 CCA retention)
- Cedar or redwood (heartwood only)
- Naturally rot-resistant species

**Composite/Synthetic Decking**:
- Composite decking with minimum 25-year manufacturer warranty
- PVC decking materials meeting ASTM D6662 standards
- Must include UV inhibitors and fade resistance

**Structural Support**:
- Posts: Minimum 4x4 pressure-treated lumber or approved equivalent
- Beams: Sized per IRC Table R507.5 based on span
- Joists: Minimum 2x6 for spans up to 8 feet, 2x8 for spans 8-10 feet
- Footings: Minimum 12 inches diameter, extending below frost line (36 inches in Springfield)

#### 4.2.4 Inspection Schedule
Required inspections for deck construction:

1. **Footing Inspection**
   - Must occur before concrete is poured
   - Verify footing depth and diameter
   - Confirm rebar placement if required

2. **Framing Inspection**
   - Must occur before decking material is installed
   - Verify structural connections
   - Check joist spacing and beam sizing
   - Inspect flashing at ledger board (if attached)

3. **Final Inspection**
   - Completed deck with railings
   - Verify railing height (36-42 inches)
   - Check baluster spacing (maximum 4 inches)
   - Confirm stairs meet code (if applicable)

#### 4.2.5 Railing Requirements
Railings required for:
- All decks more than 30 inches above grade
- All stairs with more than 3 risers

**Railing Specifications**:
- Height: Minimum 36 inches, maximum 42 inches above deck surface
- Baluster spacing: Maximum 4 inches clear between balusters
- Top rail: Must withstand 200 pounds horizontal load
- Graspable handrails required for stairs

### 4.3 Sheds and Storage Buildings

#### 4.3.1 Permit Requirements
A building permit is **required** for:
- All sheds over 120 square feet
- Any structure with permanent foundation
- Structures with electrical or plumbing connections

**No permit required** for:
- Portable sheds under 120 square feet
- Pre-fabricated sheds on skids
- Temporary storage (less than 180 days)

#### 4.3.2 Setbacks
- **Rear yard**: 5 feet minimum
- **Side yard**: 3 feet minimum
- **Front yard**: Not permitted in front setback area

#### 4.3.3 Size Limitations
- Maximum single structure: 400 square feet without fire separation
- Maximum height: 15 feet to peak of roof
- Maximum lot coverage (all accessory structures): 15% of rear yard

### 4.4 Detached Garages and Carports

#### 4.4.1 Permit Requirements
Building permit required for all detached garages and carports.

#### 4.4.2 Setbacks
- **Alley access**: 5 feet from alley property line
- **Side/Rear yard**: 5 feet from side and rear property lines
- **Front yard**: Not permitted in required front setback

#### 4.4.3 Size and Height
- Maximum area: 850 square feet for single-car, 1,200 square feet for two-car
- Maximum height: 18 feet to peak of roof
- Minimum garage door width: 8 feet per vehicle bay

## Chapter 5: Inspections

### 5.1 Inspection Request
- Inspections must be requested 24-48 hours in advance
- Request via phone (555-PERMIT) or online portal
- Site must be accessible and ready for inspection

### 5.2 Inspection Hours
- Monday-Friday: 8:00 AM - 3:00 PM
- No inspections on City holidays
- Emergency inspections available for critical issues

### 5.3 Failed Inspections
If an inspection fails:
- Inspector will provide written list of corrections required
- Corrections must be completed before re-inspection
- Re-inspection fee: $75 for third and subsequent inspections

### 5.4 Final Inspection
- Required before structure may be occupied or used
- Inspector will verify all previous corrections completed
- Certificate of Occupancy issued upon successful final inspection

## Chapter 6: Approved Materials

### 6.1 General Standards
All materials must:
- Meet or exceed current building code standards
- Be approved by ICC-ES or equivalent testing agency
- Be appropriate for intended use and climate conditions

### 6.2 Foundation Materials
**Concrete**:
- Minimum 3,000 PSI compressive strength
- Air-entrained for freeze-thaw resistance (4-7% air content)
- Maximum slump: 5 inches

**Reinforcement**:
- Rebar: ASTM A615 Grade 60
- Welded wire fabric: ASTM A185
- Placement per structural drawings

### 6.3 Framing Lumber
**Dimensional Lumber**:
- Grade: No. 2 or better for structural members
- Species: Douglas Fir, Southern Pine, Hem-Fir, or approved equivalent
- Moisture content: Maximum 19% at time of framing

**Engineered Lumber**:
- I-joists: Must meet ASTM D5055 standards
- LVL beams: Must meet ASTM D5456 standards
- Glulam: Must meet ANSI/AITC A190.1 standards

### 6.4 Exterior Cladding
Approved materials include:
- Brick or stone veneer
- Fiber cement siding (HardiePlank or equivalent)
- Vinyl siding (minimum .044 inch thickness)
- Wood siding (cedar, redwood, or treated)
- Stucco (three-coat system over approved substrate)

### 6.5 Roofing Materials
**Shingles**:
- Asphalt: Minimum Class A fire rating, 30-year warranty
- Composition: ASTM D3462 compliant
- Metal: 26-gauge minimum, appropriate fasteners

**Underlayment**:
- 15-pound felt minimum
- Synthetic underlayment per manufacturer specifications
- Ice/water shield required in valleys and eaves

## Chapter 7: Special Considerations

### 7.1 Historic Districts
Properties within designated historic districts require additional review:
- Historic Preservation Commission approval
- Maintain architectural compatibility
- Specific material and design requirements apply

### 7.2 Flood Zones
Properties in FEMA-designated flood zones:
- Elevated construction required per FEMA guidelines
- Flood vents in foundation walls
- Additional certification required

### 7.3 Energy Efficiency
New construction must meet:
- Current International Energy Conservation Code (IECC)
- Energy Star certification recommended
- Insulation values per climate zone
